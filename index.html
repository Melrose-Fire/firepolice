New York State Fire Police Authority Reference Guide

This officer is a New York State Peace Officer

Registered with the Division of Criminal Justice Services (DCJS)
Acting pursuant to special duties at emergency scenes under New York State law.

Core Statutory Authority

  • General Municipal Law (GMU) § 209-c
    Authorizes Fire Police squads and grants peace officer powers while on duty or responding on orders of the chief.
    View Full Statute
  • Criminal Procedure Law (CPL) § 2.10(41)
    Designates members of organized Fire Police squads as peace officers.
    View Full Statute
  • Criminal Procedure Law (CPL) § 2.20
    Powers of peace officers when acting pursuant to special duties include:
    Warrantless arrests, use of physical force and deadly physical force, constitutionally permissible searches, issuance of appearance tickets, and any other power authorized by law.
    View Full Statute
  • Vehicle and Traffic Law (VTL) § 132
    Fire Police qualify as “police officer” when acting pursuant to special duties. This authority supersedes traffic control devices when directing traffic at emergency scenes.
    View Full Statute

Traffic and Scene Control Authority

  • Vehicle and Traffic Law (VTL) § 1602
    Authority to regulate, direct, and control traffic at fires, accidents, and emergencies for public safety.
    View Full Statute

Authority to Address Violations at the Scene

While performing duties at an emergency scene, this officer may lawfully act on violations that affect scene safety or operations, including:

  • Failure to obey lawful traffic directions or emergency instructions (VTL §§ 1102, 1144)
    VTL § 1102 | VTL § 1144
  • Reckless driving (VTL § 1212)
    View Full Statute
  • Approaching or passing a parked, stopped, or standing authorized emergency vehicle (VTL § 1144-a)
    View Full Statute
  • Due care when approaching or passing a stationary emergency vehicle or hazard vehicle (VTL § 1146)
    View Full Statute
  • Failure to slow down or move over for emergency vehicles (VTL § 1144 and related provisions)
  • Obstructing emergency operations (Penal Law § 195.05)
    View Full Statute
  • Disorderly conduct creating safety risks (Penal Law § 240.20)
    View Full Statute
  • Leaving the scene of an incident (VTL § 600)
    View Full Statute
  • Traffic control and work zone violations including:
    VTL § 1180 (Speed restrictions) — View Full Statute
    VTL § 1102 (Obedience to traffic control devices) — View Full Statute

Traffic Enforcement at Scenes: Vehicles may be directed to stop or pull over. Failure to comply, especially when it endangers emergency personnel, can result in appropriate enforcement action.

Important Information

  • All authority is exercised pursuant to special duties at emergency incidents.
  • This officer is a registered New York State Peace Officer.
  • For verification, all statutes are linked to the official New York State Senate website (nysenate.gov).
