Traffic Control Authority

  • Vehicle and Traffic Law (VTL) § 132
    Fire Police qualify as “police officer” when acting pursuant to special duties. This authority supersedes traffic control devices when directing traffic at emergency scenes.
    View Full Statute
  • Vehicle and Traffic Law (VTL) § 1602
    Authority to regulate, direct, and control traffic at fires, accidents, and emergencies for public safety.
    View Full Statute

Emergency Scene Traffic Enforcement

While performing official duties at an emergency scene, Fire Police may direct motorists and pedestrians as necessary to ensure scene safety and maintain emergency operations.

Applicable violations may include:

  • Failure to obey lawful traffic directions or emergency instructions — VTL § 1102 | VTL § 1144
  • Reckless driving — VTL § 1212
  • Approaching or passing a parked, stopped, or standing authorized emergency vehicle — VTL § 1144-a
  • Due care when approaching or passing a stationary emergency vehicle — VTL § 1146
  • Failure to slow down or move over for emergency vehicles — VTL § 1144
  • Speed restrictions and work zone violations — VTL § 1180

Failure to comply with lawful traffic directions at and emergency scene may result in enforcement action, including the issuance of appearance tickets or arrest when authorized by law..

Arrest Authority Vehicles may be directed to stop or pull over. Failure to comply, especially when it endangers emergency personnel, can result in appropriate enforcement action, including issuance of appearance tickets or arrest under the powers granted in CPL § 2.20.

Pursuant to CPL § 2.20 and 140.25, Fire Police Officers acting within their special duties may make warrantless arrests when authorized by law.

Fire Scene Protection & Crowd Control

When operating at fires,accidents,emergencies,public events, and parades, Fire Police are authorized to:

  • Establish and maintain traffic-control points
  • Restrict access to hazardous areas
  • Keep unauthorized individuals away from the scene of emergency operations
  • Preventing theft, tampering, or inteference with emergecny apparatus, equipment and property
  • Conduct crowd control operations at fires, accidents, emergencies, and public events/parades
  • Maintain safe ingress and egress routes for emergency responders: inculding Police and tow trucks

Relevant Statutes may include:

  • Obstructing emergency operations — Penal Law § 195.05
  • Disorderly conduct creating safety risks — Penal Law § 240.20
  • Leaving the scene of an incident — VTL § 600

Important Information

  • All authority described herein is excercised only while acting pursuant to Fire Police special duties and within the scope of applicable law.
  • Members of an organized Fire Police Squad pursuant to GML 209-c are registered New York State Peace Officers.
  • For verification, all statutes are linked to the official New York State Senate website (nysenate.gov).
